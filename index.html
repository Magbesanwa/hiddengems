Birmingham's Hidden Gems

Think you know Birmingham? Bored of visiting the same places over and over again? Birmingham's Hidden Gems wants to show you that the city of Birmingham has lots of amazing bars, restaurants and cultural sites that are waiting to be explored.

This Week's Top Events »

Eat

Cosy Club Birmingham serves breakfast to late night drinks, it is the perfect place to unwind at any time with your friends. Built in 1830, this Corinthian-styled masterpiece was originally the headquarters for the Birmingham Banking Company, before being taken over by the Midland Bank in 1914. With a neo-classical theme with original detailing.

Party

The Jam House is your one stop destination for food, drink, and live music! Dusky purple lights cast romantic shadows on the walls of this funky, jazz-centric venue. Guests dine on the balcony above the main stage where soulful musicians entertain throughout the evening. This is one venue where you can eat, drink and dance to your heart's content.

Culture

The Barber Institute of Fine Arts: is a hidden gem, right on the campus of the university of Birmingham! It is a stunning art gallery and concert hall that was opened by Queen Mary in 1939.The collection is full of famous works by artists such as Vincent van Gogh, Claude Monet, Auguste Rodin, Pablo Picasso and Rembrandt, displayed in spacious and elegant galleries on the first floor of the Institute.
